Happy Ending problem

Introduction

Esther Klein a mathematician, introduced the mathematical problem that would become called the Happy ending Problem. She observed that for any given set of five points in general position, one can find a subset of four points that form a convex quadrilateral. Two of her peers at the time Paul Erdos and George Szekeres ended up working on Klein's problem in a general form. This eventualy led to the mariage of Esther Klein and George Szekeres and was there for called the happy ending problem by Paul Erdos.

K-gon or subset of k points

The convex quadrilateral mentioned in the introduction can be caled a 4-gon or a k-gon where k equals 4. Finding k-gon's became one of the main questions of Szekeres and Erdos work mentioned above. Is there a function of k that gives us the minimum size a point set N needs to be to guaranty the k-gon exists? Untill today this question is still unsolved and unproven. Ronald Graham challenges the viewer for $1000 to come up with a solution to this problem in the following Numberphile video.

Erdos-Szekeres Conjecture

Proofs exist for the problem when using the specific values 3, 4, 5 and more recently proved 6 for k. But the general form has not been proved, thus it is formulated as a conjecture, and known as the Erdos and Szekeres conjecture . Where for a k bigger then three the set of points N needs to be at least of size 2 k - 2 + 1 .

Upperbound When Calculating K-gons

The conjecture mentioned above uses the fact that a upperbound exists for the calculation of a subset of k points or k-gon. This was proven in 1935 in the original paper A combinatorial problem in geometry by Erdos and Szekeres. The proof is based on Ramsey's theorem for 2-colored hypergraphs. Since it was proven that such a upperbound exists multiple attemps to difine it happend over time.

Defining The Upperbound

Multiple attemps to proof and define the upperbounds exist, but none of all is as small as the value proposed in the Erdos-Szekeres conjecture.

Erdos-Szekeres Conjecture Lower Bound ES(k) = 2 k - 2 + 1 Erdos-Szekeres Conjecture Upper Bound ES(k) ≤ ( 2k - 4 k - 2 ) + 1


In what follows we will give a overview of best known upper bounds. This overview is based on Bedich's Thesis An Introduction to the Happy Ending Problem and the Erdos-Szekeres Conjecture. The paper explains in more detail the chronological listing that we show here.

Chung and Graham:

ES(k) ≤ ( 2k - 4 k - 2 )

Kleitman and Pachter:

ES(k) ≤ ( 2k - 4 k - 2 ) + 7 - 2k

Toth and Valtr:

ES(k) ≤ ( 2k - 5 k - 3 ) + 2

Suk:

ES(k) ≤ 2 k + o(k) + 1

This is a big improvement compared to the above and the best upper bound to date. More details on these bounds and there proofs can be found in the Authors papers. In the next and last section we will focus on the computer proof for the specific value k = 6.
